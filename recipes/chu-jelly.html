Chu Jelly: (The Legend of Zelda)

Description:

Literal slimeball enemies are a classic staple of almost every fantasy-inspired adventure game you can find. Dragon Quest, Final Fantasy, and countless other games and series have their own take on the iconic squishy enemy, and The Legend of Zelda is no exception. Zelda fans will be familiar with the tall, vibrant, googly-eyed Chuchu vacantly wandering Hyrule's varied environments, as well as the useful potions made from their colorful Chu Jelly. This simple yet creative gelatin recipe pleases both the eye and your sweet tooth.

Ingredients:

  • 3 cups Fruit Juice (I used Passionfruit but any juice will do, Apple will make it more transparent)
  • 1 cup Tonic Water (regular water can substituted if you don't want the bitterness, but tonic water will make the jelly glow in a black light!)
  • Food Dyes (Colors based on preference)
  • 4 packets Gelatin
  • Honey or other sweetener (Optional)
  • A Glass Pitcher or Funnel

Steps:

  1. Make sure you have all your ingredients ready and accessible, because we are working with hot liquid and time-senstive gelatin.
  2. Bring your juice and tonic water to a boil. If you are adding sweetener to the mix to mask the tonic taste, add it as it boils.
  3. After the liquid has reached a boil, SLOWLY stir in the gelatin with a wooden spoon, careful not to add too much at once or it may clump.
  4. Pour the liquid into the pitcher. Add the liquid from the pitcher to your bottles. If you are using a funnel, funnel the liquid directly into your bottles.
  5. Add a few drops of the food coloring you want for that particular bottle into the liquid, and stir until the color is consistent throughout the bottle.
  6. Once the bottles look right, refrigerate them for about two hours or until the jelly has become a more jelly-like texture.
